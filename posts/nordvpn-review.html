What is NordVPN?

NordVPN is a Panama-based VPN provider that has been protecting user privacy since 2012. With over 6,000 servers across 111 countries, it's one of the largest VPN networks available. It's consistently ranked as a top VPN for both speed and privacy — but does it live up to the hype in 2024?

We signed up for a real NordVPN account and tested it across Windows, Android, and browser extensions over 30 days. Here's exactly what we found.

Speed is where NordVPN really shines. Using their proprietary NordLynx protocol (built on WireGuard), we consistently retained over 85% of our base connection speed — well above the industry average of around 70%.

Privacy & Security

NordVPN uses AES-256 encryption — the same standard used by governments and militaries worldwide. But encryption alone isn't enough. What really matters is whether they keep logs of your activity.

NordVPN has a strict no-logs policy that has been independently audited three times by PricewaterhouseCoopers and Deloitte. In 2018, a server was physically seized by authorities in Finland — and there was nothing to hand over because no logs existed. That's a real-world test that very few VPNs have passed.

Key Features

Threat Protection

NordVPN's Threat Protection feature blocks malware, trackers, and intrusive ads at the DNS level — even when you're not connected to the VPN. It's a genuinely useful security layer that works surprisingly well.

Double VPN

Routes your traffic through two VPN servers instead of one for extra privacy. Slower, but useful for journalists or activists in high-risk situations.

Meshnet

Allows you to create a private encrypted network between your own devices — great for accessing your home PC remotely or gaming with friends on a private network.

6 Simultaneous Connections

One subscription covers 6 devices at once — enough for your phone, laptop, tablet, and a few others.
