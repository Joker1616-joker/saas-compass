## What is ConvertKit?

ConvertKit is an email marketing platform built specifically for content creators — bloggers, podcasters, YouTubers, and course creators. Unlike generic email tools, every feature is designed around growing an audience and selling digital products.

We used ConvertKit for 3 months across multiple projects to give you this honest review.

## Who is ConvertKit For?

ConvertKit is the best fit if you are:
- A blogger or content creator building an email list
- Selling digital products, courses, or memberships
- Someone who wants simplicity over feature bloat
- A creator who needs powerful automation without a learning curve

It's **not** the best choice if you run a large e-commerce store (use Klaviyo) or need advanced CRM features (use ActiveCampaign).

## Key Features

### Visual Automation Builder

ConvertKit's automation builder is genuinely one of the easiest to use in the industry. You drag and drop triggers, actions, and conditions on a visual canvas. Building a welcome sequence, lead magnet delivery, or product launch sequence takes minutes, not hours.

### Landing Pages & Forms

Every plan includes unlimited landing pages and opt-in forms. The templates are clean and conversion-focused. You can have a working email capture page live in under 10 minutes — no website required.

### Creator Network

ConvertKit has a built-in recommendation network where creators recommend each other's newsletters. This is a genuine list-growth tool unique to ConvertKit.

### Commerce Features

You can sell digital products and subscriptions directly through ConvertKit. No third-party tool needed for simple product sales.
